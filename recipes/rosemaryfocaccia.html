Rosemary & Garlic Focaccia Bread

Easy and tasty homemade bread perfect for beginners!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 2 cloves of Garlic, finely minced
  • 1 tbsp of fresh Thyme, minced
  • 1 tbsp of fresh Rosemary, chopped
  • 1/4 tsp of ground Black Pepper
  • 1 cup of warm Water
  • 2 1/4 tsp of active Dry Yeast
  • 1/4 tsp of Honey
  • 2 1/2 cups of All-Purpose Flour
  • 1/2 tsp of fine Sea Salt

Steps:

  1. In a cold, medium skillet, combine olive oil, minced garlic, thyme, rosemary, and pepper. Place the pan over low heat and cook, stirring occasionally, 5-10 minutes or until aromatic, but before garlic browns. Set aside
  2. In a large bowl, combine the warm water, yeast, and honey. Stir a few times then let sit for 5 minutes
  3. Add 1 cup of the flour and a 1/4 cup of the infused garlic-olive oil mixture to the bowl with yeast and honey. Stir 3 to 4 times until the flour has moistened. Let sit for another 5 minutes
  4. Stir in the remaining 1 1/2 cups of flour and the salt. When the dough comes together, transfer to a floured board and knead 10-15 times until smooth
  5. Transfer the dough to a large oiled bowl, cover with a warm, damp towel and let rise for 1 hour. It's best to the let the dough rise in a warmer area of your kitchen
  6. After 1 hour, heat the oven to 450 F
  7. Use 2 tbsp of the remaining olive oil and garlic mixture to oil a 9-inch by 13-inch rimmed baking sheet
  8. Transfer the dough to the baking sheet then press it down into the pan. Use your fingers to dimple the dough then drizzle the top with the remaining 2 tbsp of garlic olive oil. Let the dough rise for 20 minutes until it puffs slightly
  9. Bake until golden brown, 15-20 minutes. Cool baked focaccia on a wire rack
