Butternut Squash Mac & Cheese

Healthy but not boring!

This dish is perfect for a fall meal! It includes in-season produce and herbs and warms the soul just like the classic 'Mac and Cheese' dish!

Ingredients
  • 2 Bunches of Sage
  • 1 Butternut Squash
  • 1 lb. of Whole Wheat Penne Pasta
  • 4 tbsp of Butter
  • 4 tbsp of Whole Wheat Flour
  • 1 cup of Half & Half
  • 1 cup of Skim Milk
  • 1/4 cup of Grated Parmesan Cheese
  • 1/8 tsp of Cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup of Panko Breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup of Blue Cheese
  • Sriracha for finishing
Steps:
  1. Preheat oven to 425 F. Heat a medium pot of water to boiling. Pick sage leaves. Peel and medium dice the squash
  2. In a large pan, melt the butter on medium-high. Once melted, add the sage leaves and cook about 1-2 minutes or until dark green and crispy. Transfer the leaves to a paper towel, season with salt and pepper, leaving butter in the pan
  3. Sprinkle the whole wheat flour into the butter and whisk until combined. Cook on medium-high heat about a minute until golden. Whisk in the milk and/or Half and Half until no lumps remain. Reduce heat to low and season with salt and pepper. Simmer 2-3 minutes, stirring until the sauce thickens. Remove from heat
  4. While sauce simmers, add the squash to the boiling water. Cook 7-9 minutes or until very soft when pierced with a fork. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the squash to the bechamel sauce, leaving water in pot. Using a whisk or fork, mash the squash into the sauce until thoroughly combined. Stir in cinnamon, then season with salt to taste
  5. Add the pasta and a pinch of salt to the water used to cook the squash. Cook the penne until al dente. Before draining, reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water. Transfer drained penne, pasta water, and parmesan and blue cheese crumbles to the squash-bechamel mixture. Stir until well combined.
  6. Transfer the bechamel-pasta mixture into a baking dish. Drizzle the panko with olive oil and season. Sprinkle the crumbs over the casserole. Bake 5-7 minutes or until golden brown and bubbly. Crumble the crispy sage over the top and drizzle with sriracha
